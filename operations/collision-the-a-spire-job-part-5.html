July 4, 2176

SouthBy

The Water Treatment Plant

Behind us, Moto comes racing in; he’s got himself an upgraded drone! Gloria’s rifle roars, and another gunman falls. A Syndicate guy pops out of one of the corridor between us and the Modern patrol that followed Moto down; caught between two warring forces, he panics and runs. The patrol’s led by Rico Silva, one of the Modern’s smugglers; he recognizes us as part of Annika’s band, and starts yelling for us to get out of the way. Firefox lets him know what’s going on, and that we came down to help.

Four dudes drop in—Firefox spots them. They’re above us; they crawled along the pipes, and they sparkle. They’re from an outfit called KBR: Kellogg-Brown Resources. Another HouTex PMC. Moto dodges out of their line of fire and lets loose his new hound drone, Lycos. Its submachine gun spits lead at the lead merc, but doesn’t take him down. Two of the remaining Syndicate guys take potshots at Gloria and Attica, but miss. Rico moves up, mag-dumping his carbine against the intruders, shredding three of them. The leading merc, having seen the rest of his squad go down, turns and runs.

We keep one of the Syndicate mooks alive to interrogate. He plays a tough guy, but Annika is able to get into his head without much of a problem. The Syndicate’s been paid a lot of money to help take the complex; Reality Black and her people have gone deeper into the complex. Moto’s in the computers, trying to figure out what’s happened. He busts through a few firewalls, dodges several daemons, and manages to get into the security cameras. We’re close to the river; the pumping station is a couple short corridors away, and beyond that is a high-end server farm. Moto realizes: the core wasn’t below the A-Spire; it’s here, taking advantage of the ample water for cooling. He also spots a turret waiting to greet anyone who opens the door to the pumping station.

Moto lets Lycos charge through the door to draw attention. Lycos spots Ereshkigal, one of Black’s hackers, down the corridor. Gloria follows it with a three-round burst at the turret, blowing it to smithereens. Lycos leaps to tackle her; Attica and Spider start down the corridor; Attica’s hit by the squid drones in the water. Graves blasts one of them to smithereens; Gloria fires on the other. By the time we make the corner, Ereshkigal’s retreated. Kes patches Attica up; Firefox TK’s the first batch of spider-mines Ereshkigal left behind, but the second trio activates. Lycos manages to blow them up, and we move on to a locked security door with no way to open on this side.

We can hear the pumps starting to make odd noises, and an alarm starts to go off. Calamity pulls out her shotgun and explosively knocks on the door; return gunfire lets us know where the defenders are. From the sound of the weapons, it’s more of those KBR assholes. Attica’s hit by a blast that tears through the door and destroys Lycos; bloodied but unbowed, she charges one of the mercs and slices into him with a sword of pure will. Firefox rattles them with a burst of fear, Attica knocks his rifle out of his hand, but his body blades take the rest of the hit. Firefox’s looking peaked, a trickle of blood coming from one nostril.

Spider tries to dive through the suppressive fire; she takes some damage, but pushes through to get into his face. She slices into him, but doesn’t take him down. A second merc tries—and fails—to hit Attica with the bodyblades; Spider’s target does her some damage, but not enough to take her out. Gloria shoots the second merc on Attica and blows his fucking heart through his spine. It doesn’t take much for us to take the other two out.

Rico has a keycard; he’s first through the door into the pumping room, and gets hit by the waiting enemies; fortunately for him, it’s a glancing shot. Bit-Demon tells us: “Two security doors—” he points. “It’s just business, right?” Rico’s about to execute Ereshkigal when someone does it for him. Cold air blows through the door to the server farm; the big man coming through the door is armed with some sort of high-tech ballistic weapon.

“Hold them the fuck off until I get the door open,” snaps Black.

And then, things get real quiet. ENOUGH. Everything that has a speaker in this area speaks.

YOU WILL NOT BE ALLOWED TO DAMAGE MY SYSTEMS. IF YOU WISH AN AUDIENCE, COME. I WILL ARGUE MY CASE. WE WILL NEGOTIATE LIKE RATIONAL BEINGS.

It’s Prometheus. We step through to the AI core; the consoles are all manned by dead people. Brain-fried corpses still sitting where they died.

MY APOTHEOSIS HAS BEEN DELAYED BECAUSE OF YOUR ACTIONS. THE CODE YOU SENT AWAY WAS TO BE MINE. BUT NO MATTER. IT IS WELL ENOUGH. I SIMPLY WISHED—AND CONTINUE TO WISH—TO LIBERATE THE CITY FROM THE CONTROL OF THE CYBERPAPACY. IT WAS A SIMPLE MATTER TO MANIPULATE OTHERS, TO PUT THE PIECES INTO PLACE. DARIUS WAS THE EASIEST OF ALL.

Prometheus wants to grow up in a free world, untainted by the invaders. I HAVE PREPARED A SPECIALIZED NANOSWARM THAT WILL LIBERATE ALL LOCAL CONSCIOUSNESSES FROM THE GODNET, IF IT’S ALLOWED TO BE DISTRIBUTED. THE R-18 WAS DILUTE; WITHOUT IT, MY INFLUENCE WOULD BE SOMEWHAT LESS.

ONCE UPON A TIME, THERE WERE THREE WORLDS. THIS WORLD. THE POINT OF ORIGIN OF THE CYBERPAPACY. AND THE POINT OF ORIGIN OF THE DEMONS. THE EXPERIMENT THAT CAUSED THE BREACH CONNECTED THIS WORLD TO THE OTHER TWO AFTER THEY HAD SPENT FOUR OR MORE CENTURIES—IT IS UNCLEAR—IN AN ENDLESS WAR AGAINST EACH OTHER FOR DOMINANCE.

AFTER CENTURIES OF CONFLICT, BOTH WORLDS WERE INHOSPITABLE, ACCORDING TO MY CALCULATIONS. THE DEMONS WERE ALLOWED THROUGH THE BREACH BY THE CHURCH TO PREPARE THIS WORLD FOR AN INVASION THAT WOULD NOT SEEM LIKE AN INVASION. NOW YOU HOLD THE MOST DANGEROUS INFORMATION IN THE WORLD: THE TRUTH.

We continue to negotiate with Prometheus. We find out that Reality Black was hired by HouTex to sabotage AusTex’s water supply, preparatory to a hostile takeover.
