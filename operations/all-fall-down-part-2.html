March 14, 2176

AusTex: The Tombs

We got this job from Zero Ballard, who goes to the lengths of telling us that he neither likes nor trusts the client. The client wants a full investigation on one Dr. Narindar Nwolko. There’s a second job out there for a hit on a corporate exec that Nwolko did work for, so we’re going to have to play this one careful. The client’s interested in anything Dr. Nwolko might be doing that’s outside his usual routine or expertise.

We’ve got a couple leads; we can look at the guy who’s taken the job to hit the ‘zek, we can head out to Techtown and see what we can find. Moto does some investigation into Nwolko. Meanwhile, Attica makes a call—she knows one of Nwolko’s clients, Aidan Graves.

Aidan knows a lot more:

Nwolko installed reactive armor into one of the Panzer Hammers; turns out the installation didn’t take. Which got the boss of the Panzers perma-killed. This threw all sorts of things into chaos, like plans to go after the Spirit Moderns. The Spirit Moderns stole something high-powered from WET; the Panzers were supposed to be muscle for the attempt to take it back. Apparently the Panzers were getting a little above themselves, and the ‘failed installation’ might have been deliberate, creating a change in leadership. Now, the Panzers are toeing the line of their corpo masters, and getting paid in trade. And while that fight went down, the Vampire Nation got off the shot that killed the Hammers’ bossman.

In short, it’s a real mess.

AusTex: TechTown

For the immediate job, Graves can get us into TechTown without being scanned too heavily. We pull up to Nwolko’s clinic, and they’re in somewhat of a panic. That high-velocity talent from the other day, muscle from Koizumi (that may or may not be synths), is leaning on the clinic staff. Nwolko has that professional stone-face going on, though.

Graves is keeping Nwolko occupied, while Gloria and I look around. Moto’s looking through Graves’ eyes; one thing that the doctor did not do was close his files. There’s a couple dossiers: the one on the left is Zina Kova, high-powered horizontal talent for the Spirit Moderns. She’s been acting as the roper for Alistair /name/, who wants the contract for Artemis Sonata. (Who wanted us to get her special friend free from the Moderns.) The second photo is one Terra McAllister—former employee of WET. Her employment was terminated just a few days ago.

Those in the van spot some incoming heat: Verner Jackstadt; Jericho did mention that a ‘big-ass cyborg’ had called the hit job on Nwolko’s client. He leaves behind a guy in a souped-up hovercar: Daron Ocano. Kes and Spider head over to the car to see if they can exploit Ocano’s addiction to leave Jackstadt high and dry.

Meanwhile, the Koizumi guys take cover, and Nwolko politely tells Graves that they’ll need to follow up later; Graves directs us to the side door. Meanwhile, Ocano takes the bait and wanders to the van. Moto looks scruffy enough to be a dealer; when he hands Ocano a chip, Ocano just slots it and gets glitched out of his goddamned mind. Moto grins and heads over to Ocano’s float car, sliding behind the wheel…

Verner turns, slowly; sees Moto there, narrows his eyes and stalks toward the car. Moto waves cheerily and throws the car into reverse… unfortunately, Verner has nerveware. He’s fast. And he spins up his weapons…

Automated turrets spin up, blaring an alarm. WARNING! WARNING! THIS IS A SECURED AREA! LAY DOWN YOUR WEAPONS, SECURE YOUR CYBERWARE, AND DROP TO THE GROUND!

Verner’s so armored up that he ignores the first spray of light rounds, intended to tear through unprotected flesh. Something pops out of his forearm: he has a built-in combat shotgun that fires mini-grenades, and with a few shots those turrets disintegrate. His other arm pops open to reveal a razored pincer; he sprints forward with inhuman speed as the car starts to float out of his immediate reach! The Koizumi muscle scatters around to take cover, unlimbering combat rifles. Attica can’t hear anything from them; they must have internal comms. While the rest of us pile into Gloria’s van, she pulls out her own combat rifle and fires. It doesn’t do much more than sting him, but it’s enough to make him even angrier. He looks at the Koizumi guys, looks at us… and freezes. Gloria sends a three-round burst toward him, but misses; Graves pops off and manages to smash one of Verner’s optics. At that point, Verner starts running.

Everyone’s focused on Verner, nobody’s focused on us, and we get the hell out of there.

Later…

Reviewing what we got from the doctor’s office, things are about to get interesting. Both McAllister and Zina paid the doctor for various modifications, but the doctor fucked up. McAllister was supposed to get the courier drive carrying data; instead, she got a blank. Zina got the drive—but she doesn’t know she has it. Worse, the doctor managed to partially decrypt the data on the drive. It’s high-level stuff: a predictive algorithm based on certain astronomical characteristics. Specifically, the ability to predict destinations of wormholes, but it could also predict troop movements, crowd behavior, weather… it’s not a perfect Oracle, but anyone who has this thing would have one hell of an advantage over anyone who doesn’t.

Seems like someone at Koizumi got wind of the algo, hired the Panzers to take it from the Moderns, but the Panzers got ambushed and sidetracked when their top veck got perished. McAllister is the original thief, wanted the blackjack doc to install a courier drive...but somewhere in the mess, Zina got the courier drive with the white hot data installed and McAllister got a blank. Now the blackjack is sweating it because whoever wants the data is leaning on him to cough up who the thief was so they can track them down.

Problem is, McAllister doesn't have the data; Zina does, but doesn't know it. Zina's clearly planning to run away with Artemis in the name of True Love...but the Moderns want Artemis on a puppet string using Zina as a honey trap. Except that Zina's off the leash, which probably means the Moderns are hunting around for her...and the advantage they might get (assuming they could crack the drive somehow) would catapult the gang into megacorp status at least.

This will make absolutely no one happy.

How the Moderns got it, who the fuck knows? It's a bazoomy mess with not just a lot of deng involved, but power, the finest drug there is.
