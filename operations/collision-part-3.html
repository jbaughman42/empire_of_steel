AusTex: The Tombs

The Favelas

The area we’re looking in is not the greatest. We go in as a group, we go in quiet, we go in defended and armed. We decide to leave the Tastygae algae processor and the couple pieces of hustle muscle lazing around it for last.

First stop is Dr. Skin. Dog Girl is probably a Dr. Skin product; he does a lot of biotech and cosmetic. Even Skin’s security is creepy; the turrets are made of muscle and shoot teeth at people, but they still do the job. Skin denies knowing anything about the bio-roids—they’re actually insulted at the thought. The synths are far too mundane for what they create. We agree to trade some wu for information. Outside, Moto’s running drone overwatch in the van, and spots a couple gangoons meandering around. They leave Moto and the van alone, so most likely working for Skin.

The Gen-1 bioroids were fairly simple, and were probably recycled to provide raw material for the next generation. The second generation had integrated armor, communications, and were fitted with nasty lobster claws, excellent for ripping through flesh and metal. Many are fitted with a common labor exoskeleton, but the 'roid in the second-gen pic is wearing a set of Hospitaller armor. That’s not the sort of thing that gets around—and it’s usually tagged to excess. If the person who stole that armor isn’t aware of the tagging, we might be able to track it. We’ll need to talk to Sister Marie-Louise.

Next up is the black clinic. It’s guarded; Gloria tries to talk to the guards, but they’re more than a little twitchy. After a bit more conversation, Annika gets them to let us in. The medic running the clinic is Jan de Ven, a stout, no-nonsense woman. She’s still suspicious, but Annika convinces her we’re here to help. She doesn’t know anything about the synths, either.

Jan says the Church has been around more than usual; primarily investigators and tac-troops. The person in charge has been giving out QR cubes with her personal comm code, requesting she be called if there’s anything ‘unusual’. No one wants them here, but no one’s also sure what they’re looking for. Once we’re done at the clinic, Attica theorizes that the Papacy’s here hunting down a survivor of the old pogrom… it’s very hard to kill every member of a group, and harder to kill an idea.

Spider gives the rest of us a heads-up: we have Church tac-team transports coming in quiet. It’s easier for people on foot, like us, to get through the tangle of scrap and rust; de Ven’s people can watch Torrent while the lot of us go to monitor and possibly intercept them.

We watch through George’s eyes. The tac-team disembarks from their transports and starts deploying Gargoyles and Hound units. They start loudly announcing that they’re here for the people’s protection—for people to stay in their homes. In fact, they aren’t pointing guns at people—instead, they’re tacking up anti-riot barricades in front of people’s doors. Apparently with the intent to keep them safe—the barricades open from the inside. Looking at their armor, they’re from St. Michael’s—SWAT, not soldiers.

Above us, a Church gunship provides air cover—those are the Hospitallers. They’ve got a tactical dropship, armed to the teeth, providing overwatch.

A sudden commotion breaks out—in a culdesac, one of the Hounds has been broken, bleeding out shimmering hydraulic fluids. The nearby Gargoyle explodes. The nearest tac-team raises the alarm. “Contact! CONTACT! Tango sighted!”
