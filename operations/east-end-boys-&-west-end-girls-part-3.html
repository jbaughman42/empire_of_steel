July 29, 2176

Southby

Villa del Lago

This is one of those parties that, despite allegedly being for “networking”, is really just there for rich people to get their freak on. Outside, Calamity notices a peculiar ripple in the sky above the house… she spots a ship hovering above the roof. Looks like a Jacana-class dropship running a stealth package. It’s a Securitas ship. We have George keep an eye on it. Meanwhile, we’re stashing cameras. Gloria gets one in the cabana area, then tries for the exercise room. She has to divert before she’s spotted by security.

Galina, Firefox’s date, does not sugarcoat things—she’s talking about kill counts and casualties, and is really looking to make the sale. Firefox can’t quite hide her interest in what’s going on, and Galina drags her off to the library for a little tete-a-tete. Fortunately, Firefox is able to deflect Galina’s interest; they spend some time getting intimate.

Xzavier and Mudiwa are having an intense conversation about cats; this appears to piss off Oni Takamura, who rolls up on Huỳnh.

She draws him into an intense exchange. Oni’s got a transport job, and Huỳnh’s lost control of some raiders along the route she needs to travel. She’s transporting waste from Containment Zone 9 to Selma, and the contract she’s put out for additional guards hasn’t been filled. Huỳnh tries to hit on her; at her rebuff, he comments that he knows about her co-worker and her affairs.

Moto mentions something odd about the dropship; it’s heavily scanning areas of the building that should be covered by household sensors. Attica opines that it’s setting up for a kidnap or assassination; they haven’t moved yet, likely because the target isn’t here yet. There’s a possibility this is a move by Securitas to snatch Clavell; unfortunately for them, Suss claimed he wasn’t going to be there. Securitas may decide to go after Wynter, the Weytani IntSec CEO, as a fall-back. This probably isn’t a sanctioned Securitas operation.

Outside, Calamity and Moto spot three Weytani armored vehicles rolling up the drive. They’re IntSec, armed and armored with high-end gear. Attica ghosts over to the office and drops a bug there without being noticed; upstairs, Firefox isn’t so lucky. She trips on something. A spy-eye bobbles past her, apparently uncaring, but a moment later, a purple-haired girl in yellow glasses steps into the room. “Who the hell are you?” Firefox isn’t picking up thoughts off of the girl. “You need to leave. This is my area, and I’m waiting for someone.” Firefox manages to talk her way out; on her way out, she realizes that the girl is a high-end synth body double. She’s got that corpo-kiddy slumming look. This is part of Securitas’ trap—trying to draw Clavell out.

Firefox brings the security maneuvering to Galina’s attention. The KASC exec is not happy. She’s heading downstairs when the Securitas dropship drops cloak. Chaos erupts. The first two Weytani vehicles go right through the gate, and Egorva storms in with a whole group of synths. Securitas personnel are ordered to go up against the wall; everyone else is ordered to leave immediately. Outside, Calamity and Moto are ordered to show their IDs; Moto has to do some fancy hacking to make their credentials look more solid. After a thorough check, the synth returns the ID and advises that we leave now, as there’s about to be trouble.

We get out of there just as the yelling starts.

Back at the Neon Lotus, Brandy starts going through the footage. We get some good information out of it.

Takamura continues to chew out Huỳnh. She’s pissed about the rivalry between HouTex and Austex. He keeps claiming he’s not interfering with her business; she’s just collateral damage, and they’ll compensate her for that. “Look, if you want to move the waste, I can tell you who’s keeping it from being moved. Osric Kayne. He’s working with the Combinaughts, who say they can dispose of the waste without it being moved to Selma.” Osric’s in it up to his neck, Detective Kennedy is his go-between, and the “missing executive” tonight was avoiding IntSec’s attention, because he went to Space City and bought some “interesting” equipment on his personal credit line.”

Oren Strand, a Securitas exec, is one of the execs that Weytani’s people is holding. He admits this was a personal action, and tells Egorva and Wynter that if they don’t return the kids unharmed, he’ll take the evidence he has to the Securitas board and call for a full corpo war.
