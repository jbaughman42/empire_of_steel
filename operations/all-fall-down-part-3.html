March 16, 2176

AusTex: The Tombs

We give an edited version of the information we found to Zero. He says to keep ourselves available; the client is likely to want more. We advise him he may want to decline from his end, as it’s getting ugly. We do get the payout, plus the location of a stash.

The stash is in Dome Town, at Violator. It’s the size of a full-size modular shipping container. There’s a cash vault—the number on it is 14.4K, some collectibles, a couple crates of current-gen Colonial-issue MREs (better rats than a lot of people eat right now). Below the container is a highly-shielded secondary container that’s set up as a small safe-house. It’s exceedingly small, because part of that safe-house includes a clone tank.

There’s also a single-use storage device: on it is a dossier. Combining the information in the dossier with the existence of both the algo and the clone tank suggests that Zheng was intending to create a flesh computer, a super-processor with an organic substrate.

We’re united in needing to destroy the algorithm. The easiest way is to simply kill Zina, but that’s a little distasteful. But Artemis Sonata’s job gives us an in. Rescue Zina from the Moderns.

We meet Artemis before her protest concert in front of the SleepDealers. Zina’s going to be at the show; Artemis is worried about that, because she thinks it means the Spirit Moderns are up to something. Zina says she was supposed to act as a middleman, hand off incriminating information to some guy named Ocano. That’s probably why the brainware was installed—Artemis thinks it’s a brainlock.

Artemis has a second request: she’s worried about the Panzers rolling up, but they’re too busy with their own leadership struggle. If anyone’s gonna roll up on the show, it’ll be the Moderns. Vampire Nation, possibly, just because they love music, but the Moderns might well try to crash the party and give her an offer she can’t refuse.

We agree to help with security. Attica and Graves walk the site, while Moto goes and gets himself a new off-the-shelf drone. The problem is… someone managed to stuff an AI in there. Named George.

George, it turns out, is a combat veteran, an expert system that became self-aware after a virus in the Somali War. It’s willing to do its part, and is extremely offended at Moto’s suggestion that he might flee.

Spider gets in touch with an old contact in the Moderns, and found out that her old boss, Delia Graydon, is going to be there.

The concert site’s a nightmare; it’s technically outdoor, but surrounded by urban blight. There’s too many ways someone could take a shot at Artemis, but we’ll do our best. The Moderns have set up cameras around the whole place, and they’re probably intending to create bootlegs. The data feed is going to Southby; there’s an indie studio called Skinjob Records just around the corner that the Moderns are clearly trying to undermine.

Zina’s here, escorted by Wynston Lien, watching from one of the rooftops; on another rooftop are members of the Vampire Nation. Not just any members, either: the Prince of the Vampire Nation herself, Nastasia Gwinelle and her retinue. This could spell trouble…

And does. Wynston’s on some narcosoft, and aggressive with it. One of the Vamps starts some trouble—he’s got Presence and Dominate mods installed, trying to back Wynston down. He’s not going to back down, though Zina freezes in panic. Attica hops over to the Prince’s balcony and manages to negotiate them into peace; unfortunately, Wynston decides to throw his weight around and Zina laughs at him. He shoves her back into the crowd of Moderns behind him and tells them to ‘have some fun’. Attica and Spyder move to intervene; George also moves in, but follows Wynston when he gets the call that we’ve interrupted their bootleg stream.

Gloria finally manages to dial in Delia; she’s on the balcony between the Moderns and the Vamps. And as the first Modern lays an unwelcome hand on Zina… shit gets real. Gloria blows a hole in the mob by blowing a hole through a Modern ganger. Attica uses the hole to pull Zina out of the crowd of her assailants, dropping down between two of the buildings to keep her out of Delia’s line of fire, as well. George ‘accidentally’ hits Wynston in the head, knocking him off the balcony in an unconscious heap. The SleepDealer guards run to his aid. Spyder decides to have some fun, gutting a second Modern.

Delia drops down, whispers “Spoiler!” and throws a grenade, and it’s placed too perfectly, and Attica’s just that instant too slow to protect Zina from it; Attica’s mildly injured, but Zina’s badly hurt. At that point, Delia leaps back up to the balcony to try to run. Gloria sights in at Delia; the bullet vaporizes her left leg. She collapses, and before the other Moderns can come to her aid, the Vamps dart in and drag her off.

We get Zina extracted to the van so Kes can start first aid; Gloria sends an address to Calamity and puts the lead on for the frantic run down to her hideout. It’s a rough road, but Kes is able to keep Zina alive just long enough to get her to the surgery. For just a moment, she flatlines… but Kes is finally able to stem the bleeding and stabilize her.

She’s not going to be stable for any kind of surgery for at least a month... but the drive is intact.

Artemis says, “I never thought the Moderns would go so far. It wasn’t your fault—who’d have thought she’d throw a grenade?”

The news comes on; the Spirit Moderns are being blamed for setting off a bomb at the concert. AusTex Response rolled in just after we bugged out.
