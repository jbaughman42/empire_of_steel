March 13, 2176

AusTex: The Tombs

The leader of the Panzer Hammers has been killed by the Vampire Nation. There’s a lot of infighting as the gang tries to decide who the new leader’s going to be. This is causing general unrest.

We’re all hanging out at the local Tombs dive bar, the Knife Shop. There’s a few jobs going around; we end up picking one up from Henry Jericho, one of the local fixers.

The client is Oliver Gaskell. He’s an ex-con who has a contact who desperately needs to get out of the AusTex area. The principal is holed up in the old ReFood in Sector N7. (We’ve eaten there, and lived to regret it.) The job is simple, get him out and to the RV point. The complicated part is that he’s under siege from corporate agents and they have heavy drone coverage. Another complication is that ATR has a copshop nearby, in sector N9.

Our contact, Mittens, wants to meet us at the Grand Metropol Hotel. The Presidential Suite. The elevator smells of burning insulation and industrial lubricants; the cybrids here are not well-maintained, and they have a tendency to glitch. It’s not a pleasant situation for any involved. The cybrids on duty watch us with dull yet enhanced eyes.

“Need human man. Human man know where my human man is. My human man is late. Need scritch. St!nkman after other man. Jericho say other man (Gaskell) in Refood. Other man supposed to—did not—help human—my human. I lab assistant. Very good assistant. Is work on silly thing, but important for hoo-man.” She lets out an exasperated sigh. “Very important for hoo-man.”

“St!nkman come to door; Mittens take picture. St!nkman now flat.” St!nkman is a freelance agent named Lloyd Ashworth. Crazy Lloyd. Lloyd’s the agent on the ground looking for the principal. He’s currently sitting in an armored sedan, probably on loan from whatever corp he’s working for. The sedan has a drone bay, entirely empty—looks like it could hold four drones. Lloyd looks pissed. He looks as if he’s trying to figure out exactly what stupid thing he’s about to do…

March 14, 2176

AusTex: The Tombs

Lloyd looks like he’s about to blow his top, so we need to move fast. Spider and Kes go in the front, Attica goes in the back, and Moto and Calamity are in the truck. One of Lloyd’s drones investigates them; they play dumb, lost civvies. A second drone spots Attica in the back; she manages to lose it by going inside the back door. Abruptly, two of Lloyd’s drones start acting odd; zooming off in various directions, giving us an opportunity.

While the rest of the team is getting Oliver Gaskell out, Attica saunters over to Lloyd’s car—turns out he’s being blackmailed by the corp, and isn’t real thrilled about being forced to work for whoever it is. Scuttlebutt has it that Lloyd is working for the corps, a job gone wrong. He tried to lean on Earl for something—whatever it was, the corps felt it was worth an international incident—but it wasn’t violent. Meanwhile, we’re getting Oliver out to where there’s supposed to be a hidden car near the Aerospace port. We get him there, and he says he owes us one. And just as a bit of advice, he says to avoid Koizumi Aerospace Corporation—whatever it is they want to buy, it’s hot. The talent they had earlier were high-velocity, possibly even Synths.
