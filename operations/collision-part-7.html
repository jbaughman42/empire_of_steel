July 4, 2176

AusTex: The Tombs

Abandoned Carnival

A turret on top of the WeYu AV pops out to reveal a massive 20mm cannon; it fires… at the transpo van. Anything coming out of it (assuming we’d left anyone alive in there) would be thoroughly suppressed. The rest of them spread out, taking cover nearby. One of them disappears, while multiple gun drones dart out of the AV. Yet another trooper pops smoke in long, unnatural lines—some sort of nanoparticulate, polarized so they can see through it and we can’t. Bullets punch through the smoke wall; Attica manages to writhe out of their way, but Graves and Calamity are hit, as is de Ven and one of Misha’s people. The unconscious de Ven is shredded. Calamity, too, goes down—mostly flesh wounds, but she’s bleeding badly. Graves is pounded, but he just bleeds and roars and keeps on shooting.

Another trooper blasts Annika with a shotgun, while the AMP operative blows out a wave of concussive force combined with EMP. George makes an error noise and grows some holes, but isn’t quite down. Finally, the strike clone opens up with the minigun on Gloria; Gloria’s also hit badly.

Gloria hops the waist-length fence and somehow vanishes; she takes cover behind her van and opens up on the drone rigger. Saad, in Spider’s grip, gasps out that it’s a retrieval team; we suspect it was a WeYu sting aimed at Hernandez’ people. Time to pull the fuck out.

We get back to one of Annika’s safe houses, where Kestrel is waiting; she pushes everyone out of the way and gets to work on the wounded. First Calamity, then Graves, Annika and Gloria; she gets them all patched up.

We need to have a brief meeting with Hernandez, clear the books, before our meet with Reality Black. Hernandez confirms that Saad was laying a trap—the weapons were stolen from a Weytani depot. Weytani doesn’t take that kind of loss lightly, so they sent in a goon squad to punish the thieves. In a completely unrelated piece of news, the Hungry for God church is a smoking hole; Weytani probably strafed it after the Church did their thing. Church forces and the police are on the lookout for one Dr. Poirot; citizens are advised to avoid him if he’s seen.

We also make sure Tanit Saad is thoroughly burned, her corporate credentials spread to everyone we know.

July 6, 2176

Mutilator

The meet with Reality Black takes place at Mutilator. We can’t afford to wait longer on this; the opportunity to take down the Spirit Moderns while they’re weak won’t come again. Black’s crew mostly waits at the bar—Ereshkigal, Citizen Sabotage, Bit Fiend, Silver Oni.

The Moderns have brought in some extra talent to beef up their security, because Bishop Devin from down south is constantly protesting, and, one assumes the Church is conducting cyber attacks. The Church has a European bishop in to help them. Darius Vasser is getting paranoid; his security chief, Enzo Blackford, has brought in some top-tier cyber talent from India known as Algol. Algol is an infosec specialist; she may be working for the Church on the down-low.

Black thinks if we can get into the central data core, we can release the locks on the springs. Above all else, though, we need to keep the Church out of Southby; if they get control of the water, they could start dosing the population with any number of things.

The Pyramid is hardened and air-gapped; it has its own on-site power generation, a combination of fusion and aerostat. We also want to limit civilian casualties; most of the people working there don’t have a choice. We’re the distraction team; Black’s team will do the primary infiltration.

People are reluctant to talk about the Pyramid, and for good reason, but Annika has her ways. Compromising the staff isn’t going to happen; they’re all junkies, addicted to substances they get as part of their employment, and the Moderns have ways of keeping them on the hook. It’s a combination of drugs, conditioning, etc. There’s a generalized drug distribution system—aerosols—that’s the pharma equivalent of MSG. It makes everything more effective. They have their own hospital inside. The aerosol system is highly secured, and Vasser will sometimes just fuck with it for fun.

Security depends entirely on how much you’re paying. Everyone gets an RFID chip, so they can track you. The guards are universally enhanced: chrome, bio, a combination of both. There’s even a few amps on staff—their identities are kept carefully secret.

Annika has an idea: the Pyramid regularly hires bands to perform. Annika calls up her old band; they could be our way in. Between the band and the dancers, we can create a distraction. Moto can spoof the RFID chips. It’s also rumored that the Moderns have the capability to cause everybody in areas that they control to go berserk—and even direct them.

We decide we’re going to have to go into the public areas, see what we can find out.
