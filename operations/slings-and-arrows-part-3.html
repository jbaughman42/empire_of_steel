April 6, 2176

AusTex: Bryker Woods

Final preparations are made, and we decide to sneak in through the tunnel from the abandoned admin complex. It comes out in a maintenance area beneath the airpad. Moto does his magic on the computer systems, pulling up the cameras on the maintenance area. He registers three clear heat signatures, and two more stealthed on the catwalk.

Attica sneaks forward to eavesdrop. The two on the catwalk are Quinn Jackstadt and Isis Wakeman; they’re arguing about an “upgrade” to the site’s defenses. Turns out they have something called an Eversor Walker—a light assault mech. It’s a bullet hose. Quinn’s pissed about it being there; since the Panzers are no longer going to be conducting their assault, there’s no reason for it to be there. (Quinn’s also not real happy about being in charge of the black site.) The new construction we were worried about is apparently its storage facility.

To get to Sector Five, we need a small distraction; Moto brings up a small roach drone and starts fiddling with the VTOL. It starts making some very unpleasant sounding noises, like it’s got the electronic equivalent of epilepsy. That distracts the mechanics enough for us to sneak across to the Sector Five entrance…

We use our shiny fake passcards to get past the security booth when Moto pings us urgently. “Guys? Guys… they’ve rotated the keys…” Moto works fast to secure the turrets—just in case—while Firefox and Graves talk us through the gates. A coincidental? system malfunction works in our favor, and the security guards buy it. We’re waved on through; Moto’s still got control of the systems, and after a database search to locate our target, we move.

Unfortunately, there’s no predicting the human element. A cop steps out of the security office—the name of ‘Keefe’ on his uniform—and on seeing us, he steps back into the office. Alarms start going off. Graves flings a canister of crash foam at the office door, blocking it; Attica manages to break the electromagnetic lock into the VR cell block. Moto tries to make it look like a false alarm, but the cops aren’t buying it. Graves dashes down to jack in and start the wake-up process, while Attica jams the northern door. Calamity hears Jackstadt coming, and warns us in time for Moto to prevent them from locking down the elevator. Whoever’s on the northern door is about to break through; Firefox manages to weld it shut, while Attica heads to the other end of the corridor, tossing a gas grenade. Unfortunately, the first two responders have breathers and aren’t affected; they come out of the gas and hose Attica and Calamity down! Two more guards come out on the first floor, spotting Graves. Kestrel does her medical magic to jumpstart McAllister’s waking process.

Graves starts jacking out as Firefox steps out on the catwalk and lights on fire, snarling, “If you don’t want to get burned, get out of the way!” They don’t run, surprisingly; they start yelling “AMP! AMP!" They pull back, but in good order. Moto gives us our exit directions; Attica jams the sliding door between them and the oncoming Response team. Calamity takes one of Attica’s smoke grenades and tries to toss it down the corridor; it bounces and goes off… elsewhere.

One of their bots comes charging up, slamming into the jammed door; it doesn’t knock it down, but the grate is now a tangled mess. The heavy weapons guy readies to fire HE on the door, while downstairs the response team rolls a SLAG grenade through the door. Moto opens the doors between us and the pad, and we begin a fighting retreat. Jackstadt’s directing an attempted intercept, while Pro-Cop’s own Amp throws a telekinetic bomb our way. We throw a little more crash foam for protection and make a break, as Moto seals the doors along the response team’s route. There’s still cops along our exit route, though, and they start shooting! It’s a clean miss, though—and Spider doesn’t give them a second chance! She rips through one of them, leaving him bleeding out, while Gloria rampages behind her—the remaining cops scatter while Attica seals the door behind us!

There’s only one obstacle left: the mech. George distracts it long enough for us to lay down Attica’s last two smoke grenades, and we make a run for the tunnel. The mech’s barely small enough to fit in the tunnel behind us—it simply lays down fire. It’s not terribly accurate; a few of us take some fragments, and Graves drops his last crash foam canister. We make it to Torrent without further damage, as George joins us. Calamity’s usual skill behind the wheel gets us out and merged into traffic, where we simply disappear.
