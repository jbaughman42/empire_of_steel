July 4, 2176

AusTex: The Tombs

Abandoned Carnival

There’s nothing good about carnivals, and even less about burned-out, abandoned carnivals. So it makes sense that creepy cultists are going to be using this as a meet point.

We get the low-down on the flash team. They’re working for Misha Fernandez of The Circuit, put together by the (allegedly) ex-corpo fixer Tanit Saad.

  • No one likes working with Emma Baurrfau; she’s wired to the gills with speedware and is a psycho razorgirl
  • Jules Fillion is considered one of the toughest operators in the AusTex area; he just doesn’t die
  • Terminal Conflict has been rumored to have blown the last couple decking jobs they’ve taken, for reasons unknown
  • Eymeric Richara has an unfortunate street name—Jank—due to the fact that his chrome is janky. They are the relative of someone fairly high in the criminal scheme of things in AusTex proper
  • Catrine Duchamp is a face, not a fixer, and she’s in debt due to a very expensive drug habit

The thing they have in common is: they’re all financially desperate.

There’s a run-down motel outside the carnival; looks like someone’s set up there. We do some investigation into which crew’s set up. Annika sends out a telepathic probe… and senses bio-roids. Those are the buyers.

George goes out to recon the carnival itself, and finds the sellers. Duchamp’s set up on the ferris wheel in a sniping position, packing some serious heat. Several of the others are on monobikes. We decide the safest bet is to try to warn the sellers; if we can get them on our side, taking down the bio-roids will be a lot easier.

Jules is the one taking point for the sellers, though it’s clear he’s on the comms with someone else. Annika does the negotiating on our end—fortunately, nobody sane likes bio-roids, and we point out that just because we’re not getting a payout doesn’t mean we don’t have a reason to go after those things. They invite us in, and we get set up near the coaster just a few moments before the buyer shows up.

In his van full of bio-roids.

We recognize the driver: Jan de Ven. She starts to talk, starts to show a credvault—and then she recognizes us. And her eyes flicker.

Attika leaps into action; in an instant she has Jan de Ven on the ground unconscious. The client, Fernandez, pulls out in a panic; we ignore her. A vehicle drops down from the omnipresent cloud cover—it’s a Wutani AV. It circles the area, but doesn’t as yet do anything. And then, the bio-roids—no longer under de Ven’s control—pour out of the van. They’re armed with all sorts of interesting weapons, and smart enough to take at least partial cover. They’re still moving under direction; it looks like de Ven wasn’t the primary controller. Could be one of the bio-roids itself, or possibly someone in the Wutani ‘craft.

Atop the ferris wheel, Catrina cuts loose with the M24; she’s blasting holes in the ‘roids in a combination of suppression and area effect. Annika stares at Flamethrower ‘Roid… and sets him on fire. The tanks of his flamethrower go boom. Several of the bio-roids collapse, dead. Two more are incapacitated. At that point, the AV slams down to the ground behind the ‘roids. Two of the bio-roids are still up; one turns and runs for the fence, apparently having more brains than his buddies. Then he runs for Gloria’s van.

Emma blasts the other remaining bio-roid into chunky salsa, and the AV disgorges a black-clad WeYu EEL team. Meanwhile, Gloria’s not happy that someone’s going after her van, and corpsifies him with a three-round burst. Inside Fernandez’ van, Misha and Saad are having some sort of argument. Spider dashes to the van, knocks on the windows with her claws, and says, “Play nicely, my friends?” The two women are definitely not playing nicely, so Spider yanks Saad out of the van and slams her down on the ground to the sound of Fernandez screaming “Fucking puta bitch!” after Saad. She hops out of the van, pointing her gun right at Tanit’s face. “Fucking sell me out to WeYu? You’re gonna fuckin’ die.”
