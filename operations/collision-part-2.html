AusTex: The Tombs

Cradle Biolabs Agristacks

We decide to start with the disappearances. The contact, Abel Kane, is out by the Veravas - Cradle Biolabs Agristacks, and that’s Panzer Hammers territory. We’re walking carefully; don’t want to bring trouble down.

Kane’s a combat vet; he and Gloria bond over shared combat traumas. Kane would ordinarily handle something like this himself, but this is different. Kane tells us it’s not the Papacy—they’re dropping in heavy-duty aerodynes, drones, the works. Ordinarily they aren’t so obvious; there’s a few missionaries in the district, but they tend to keep a low profile.

Favela Attackers


Bio-roids

Abel got a look at the attackers via a low-res camera: they appear to be bio-roids of some sort, low-chrome, with an odd symbol on their heads. 3D-printed androids, made from organic slurry. They’re mass-printed without a full brain, though some can have an organic brain with cyberimplants. And cybereyes. They were (and in some places, are) used offworld for cheap labor; more typically, they’re used as cannon fodder. They have no sense of self-preservation outside the mission. In this case, the mission is clearing out the favelas and kidnapping people. Even worse, they have stunted immune systems; if there’s a disease around, they’ll catch it and become super-spreaders.

The process of printing off these things is simple: you need a small amount of nanites and sufficient amounts of biomaterial. We could do it with our clone tanks. The bottlenecks are biomass and the creation of a template.

Attica is utterly certain that these aren’t Cyberpapacy troops—this completely goes against the Cyberpapacy’s beliefs. These are an abomination of the flesh—men playing God.

The advantages: they can be cranked out by the job lot once you have the template; they can’t be hacked; they’re inexpensive, and they are utterly loyal.

There’s one gang in the area that might know something about the tech needed to create these things: the Vampire Nation. We contact Tallisi to set up a meeting; it won’t be until later in the evening.

The Stacks

A capsule hotel turned into a retirement home. Everything one expects of such a place. A lot of people crammed into too small a space, but a lot of them are old-school punks and highly unlikely to do something like narc.

Simone Church is in the basement; allegedly, this is where the cheap coffins are. We walk through a line of fake coffins and come into a secure, discreet meeting room. Her first words are: “Why the fuck is she here?” — referring to Attica. Attica knows her—they used to be cloistered sisters in the same convent. Church was part of the Order of St. Paul (the Caduceus), while Attica was in the Hospitallers. Attica explains that she got out—eighteen months ago.

The church has decided they’re going to co-opt the Modern’s sysadmin. They figure with two of the Moderns’ people down, now’s the time to move in. They’ve got a small force elsewhere in the Tombs, looking into something. They’re led by Sistrix Radoslava Barsamian, an investigator with the Order of St. Michael.

The Church experimented with bio-roids once; it went so epically wrong that an entire order of the church was destroyed, root and branch. Hospitaller-Captain Zimmerman is another fanatic—and another gifted such as Attica. He was part of the pogrom. Attica wonders if some member of the order may have escaped the pogroms. Simone hopes not—the head of the order was beyond heretical, going into insane.

Meeting with the Vampire Nation. The club is less crowded than usual, but the music is loud and the dancers are happy. The Prince is at the back; white sound generators are placed at strategic intervals to interfere with enhanced hearing. It’s probably not real blood that showers from the ceiling.

Prince Nastasia Gwinelle is waiting for us—she has already ensured our privacy, and invites us back into a secured room. Firefox outlines the situation with the bio-roids, and respectfully inquires into what the Nation might have heard regarding sites that might be suitable for setting up a bio-roid factory.
